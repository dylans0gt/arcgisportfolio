Fishing Safety Analysis

In this demonstration, I illustrated how consumption warnings and advisories regarding fishing are likely to be implemented in areas near harmful algal blooms within Morris County, New Jersey. By incorporating the layer of NJ trout-stocked streams, I aimed to emphasize the prevalence of seemingly optimal fishing spots that may harbor fish unsafe for consumption due to environmental factors.

Tools: ArcGIS online, Wordpress

Printing Madison

For this project, I digitized the location of a handful of printers in the suburb of Madison, New Jersey. Using a free, legal website called Wigle, I collected data from 55 randomly-selected WiFi networks and put them into a spreadsheet. I then filtered out the networks with the word "print" or another related term in the SSID (network name) and put them into a separate spreadsheet. With that data, I made a Google My Map showcasing the coordinates of the printers. I then digitized the buildings in which those printers can be found using ArcGIS pro.

Tools: Google Sheets, Wigle WiFi, Google My Maps, ArcGIS Pro

Routing Madison

For this project, I took a similar approach to the 'Printing Madison' project. However, this time around, I switched gears and targeted routers instead of printers. My goal was to identify routers with either no encryption or weak WEP encryption. I then flagged these vulnerable routers to shed light on one of the myriad tactics hackers can use to exploit a geographic area through basic router attacks."

Tools: Google Sheets, Wigle WiFi, Google My Maps, ArcGIS Pro

Common Raven Sightings

For this mini project, I received a comprehensive dataset from eBird detailing bird species sightings in Madison, NJ, along with their respective frequented locations. After isolating data specifically related to "Common Ravens," I transferred it to a separate spreadsheet. Subsequently, I imported this refined dataset into ArcGIS Pro software, where I visualized it as a heatmap to effectively represent sighting frequencies. To ensure clarity and avoid any potential misinterpretations associated with emergency connotations, I adjusted the color scheme from the standard red/yellow to blue/green, as the former hues often evoke urgent connotations.

Tools: Google Sheets, eBird, ArcGIS Pro

RibbitReport

RibbitReport is your go-to platform for amphibian enthusiasts across the United States to share their sightings of frogs, salamanders, and other fascinating amphibians. Whether you're a seasoned herpetologist or simply passionate about nature, RibbitReport empowers users to contribute to a collective database of amphibian observations. With easy sorting options by state and zip code, users can swiftly locate sightings in their vicinity or explore amphibian activity across the nation. Join our community and become part of a nationwide effort to document and appreciate these captivating creatures!

Tools: VSCode
Languages: Python, SQL
Frameworks: Flask, SQAlchemy
